# CINEMATOGRAPHY — shot, movement & fight language (learn before prompting scenes)

Companion to `VIDEO-PROMPT-LESSONS.md`. This is the vocabulary the video prompts must use so the
motion is intentional and cinematic, plus a per-beat shot plan for the FORP hero intro (N=17).

## Shot sizes (frame the emotion)
- **EWS/ELS** extreme wide — scale/isolation (the ultimate sun, the shatter).
- **WS/LS** wide — full body in space, staging the fight.
- **MS** medium (waist-up) — the identity/hero default; face stays large & sharp (see image L9/L24).
- **MCU/CU** close / extreme close — a detail: the disc forming, the evil-eye, embers on skin.
- **OTS** over-the-shoulder — him vs the hooded attacker. **POV** — rare, for a strike landing.

## Camera movements (ONE primary per clip; name stability)
- **Push-in / dolly-in** — build intensity toward him or the disc (calm, charge).
- **Pull-out / dolly-out** — reveal scale/aftermath (settle, ultimate).
- **Pan / tilt** — reframe on a fixed axis (follow a thrown disc; tilt up to the sun).
- **Truck / crab** — lateral track parallel to him (dodge, moving fight).
- **Arc / orbit** — circle him keeping focus (shatter, flame-inside reveal).
- **Crane up/down** — lift for the god's-eye ultimate; drop into the settle.
- **Handheld / whip** — energy & impact (the interrupt hit); use sparingly, it fights our controlled grade.
- **Long take (oner)** — our whole intro reads as one continuous scroll; keep moves smooth & motivated.
- Stability words: **tripod (locked)**, **gimbal (smooth glide)**, **handheld (alive/shaky)**.

## Fight / action language
- **Beat** = one action+intent (throw, block, hit, recoil). Nail the move AND its emotion.
- Techniques to name: throw/hurl, spin, slip, bob & weave, parry/block, jump-back (evade), stagger/recoil,
  charge/wind-up, impact, follow-through, aftermath. **Flow** = continuous pacing between beats.
- Motion physics to describe: mantle flares, shards tumble, embers drift, knees buckle on impact,
  torso twists from a hit, motion-blur on fast strikes. Prefer **slow/continuous/natural** over
  exaggerated/high-speed (seedance distorts on the latter).
- 180° axis: keep him and the attacker on consistent screen sides across beats (no jarring flips).
